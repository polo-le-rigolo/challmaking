Glitching instructions

1. Connect UART correctly on the PCB

2. Configure the multimeter (figure out what you want/need to mesure for your glitch)

3. Interact with the glitcher via HTTP:

   POST /run_code
   { "code": "help()" }

   Example:
   curl -X POST http://challenge-instance:port/run_code \
        -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
        -d '{"code":"get_pin_voltage(\"uart_tx\")"}'

  The glitch() function will first cause the device to reboot and glitch the pin at t=delay, t=0 being the boot, for a duration of t=duration.
  The delta between t=0 and t=glitch (moment where the glitch should happen) is constant.
  Everything is in ns (both delay and duration). Our experts determined that, past 300ns, the device has already disabled the uart debugging and that the instruction takes less than 15 ns to execute itself.

4. Connect to UART via netcat and let the device boot to get your flag :p
